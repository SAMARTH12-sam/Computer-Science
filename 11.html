Newton's Laws and Equations of Motion

Visualizing displacement, velocity, and acceleration under constant acceleration.

Newton's Three Laws of Motion

First Law (Inertia): An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a force.
Proof: From F = ma, if F = 0, then a = 0. Velocity stays constant: v = u.

Second Law (Acceleration): Force equals rate of change of momentum.
Proof: F = dp/dt = m dv/dt = ma. This leads to the equations of motion.

Third Law (Action–Reaction): For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
Proof: Conservation of momentum gives F_AB = -F_BA.

Equation 1: v = u + at

Equation 2: s = ut + ½at²

Equation 3: v² = u² + 2as

Worked Example

Given: u = 20 m/s, a = 5 m/s², t = 4 s

Equation 1: v = u + at = 20 + (5×4) = 40 m/s

Equation 2: s = ut + ½at² = (20×4) + ½(5)(16) = 120 m

Equation 3: v² = u² + 2as = 400 + 1200 = 1600 → v = 40 m/s

✔ All equations agree!
